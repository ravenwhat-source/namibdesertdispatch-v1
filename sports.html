Capricorn League Kicks Off With Big Hopes for Young Netball Players

By Launa Muunda | 20 May 2025

Photo: The Economist

The Capricorn Schools Netball League has officially started, bringing excitement to schools across Namibia. This year, 85 school teams from different parts of the country are taking part, showing just how much the league has grown.

More than just a sports competition, this event gives young girls the chance to build confidence, learn teamwork, and stay active. The league includes players from as young as 12 to those in their final years of school. Matches will be played over the next few months, leading up to the final games in August.

What makes this league special is that it is not only about winning,it is about helping girls grow on and off the court. Sponsored by Capricorn Group and supported by the Namibian School Sport Union, the league encourages values like hard work and commitment. Many teachers and coaches also see it as a way to teach discipline and leadership through sport.

Since it began in 2017, the Capricorn Netball League has helped hundreds of girls fall in love with the game. With support from both schools and communities, it continues to give young athletes a space to shine and to dream big.
