Selma Kamanya’s Show-Stopping Outfit to the World

Written by Ngapurue Murangi | 25 May 2025

Photo Credit: Miss Universe Namibia

WINDHOEK, MAY 25 — Namibia took centre stage at the Miss World African Qualifiers 2025 as Selma Kamanya’s national costume sent waves.

Designed by local fashion designer Naemi Paulus, the costume — titled “Omaano Kalunga”, which means “Gift of God” — represented the Namibian sun and paid tribute to Namibian cultures like the Ovahimba and Oshiwambo.

For a local designer, this is a milestone as her craftsmanship is displayed and exposed to the world. It is more than just fashion; it is an opportunity. This motivates young and upcoming designers to dream bigger and stay rooted in their identity while reaching global heights.

Namibia’s fashion industry is still a developing layer; such visibility will boost talented designers with confidence and positivity. Doors to collaborations, investments, and worldwide recognition are opened.

Selma’s outfit reminds the world that Namibia has stories to tell, and the designers are here to enlighten the world through their unique talents and designs.
